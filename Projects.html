Online damage detection using time reversed guided waves in doubly curved shells


- Time reversed lamb wave propagation-based structural health monitoring technique utilizes piezoelectric actuators to ultrasonically excite the structure and transmit back the time reversed response signal through the receiver to the original transmitter location.
- It is the process of reconstructing the original input signal at the source by emitting the time-reversed response signal recorded at another point back to the source location.
- The work aims to use the time-reversal lamb wave technique (TRLW) for damage detection on a doubly curved shell (Material- AA2219), which is a part of the PSLV Liquid Propulsion Tank.
- Transducers array is used for the experiment.
- From experimental results, it is found out that the TRLW technique can be efficiently used for doubly curved shell without any baseline data from the undamaged structure.

Optimization of Multiple Traveling Salesman Problem


- The objective was to minimize the total distance traveled by all the salesmen.
- Objective function, decision variable and constraints were decided according to the problem definition.
- A mathematical formulation for the mTSP is presented and solutions for a certain number of salesperson visiting multiple cities (51 cities problem, 11 cities and 12 cities) is obtained using CPLEX optimization solver.
- The results obtained were compared with the Literature.

Modeling and Control of Vehicle dynamics system


- Modeled 4 DOF system for vehicle dynamics.
- Descritized and linearized the system.
- States (position and orientation errors) were calculated for velocity and steering inputs.
- Using states, trajectory of the vehicle in XY frame was identified.
